# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud Targeting College Students

**The Crime:** UPI Payment Fraud via Social Engineering.

**How it Happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. A college student lists an old textbook or a piece of furniture (like a study table) for sale on a platform like OLX or a college Facebook group.
2. The scammer poses as an eager buyer and agrees to the price immediately, without any negotiation.
3. The scammer claims they are sending the money via UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) but states they have a "merchant account" or are using a "QR code scanner" for the transfer.
4. The scammer sends a QR code or a payment request link to the student and tells them to "enter their UPI PIN to receive the money."
5. Unsuspecting students, rushing to complete the sale, scan the code or click the link, enter their PIN, and the money is instantly deducted from their bank account instead of being credited. 

**Who is Targeted:** College students are frequent targets because they often buy and sell used goods online, frequently use digital payments, and may be less experienced with the subtle tricks scammers use on these platforms.

**Consequences:**
The victim suffers immediate financial loss, which can be devastating for a student managing a tight budget or paying college fees. Additionally, the student may experience anxiety, loss of trust in digital platforms, and the stressful process of filing police and bank reports to attempt to recover the funds.
