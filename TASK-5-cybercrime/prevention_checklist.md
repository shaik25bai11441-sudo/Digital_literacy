# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

1. **Never share your UPI PIN to *receive* money.** Your PIN is strictly for sending money or checking your balance.
2. **Verify the receiver's details before paying.** Always double-check the VPA (Virtual Payment Address) and the name that pops up on your UPI app before hitting send, especially when paying college fees or buying items online.
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).** Turn this on for all critical accounts: email, GitHub, LinkedIn, and banking apps.
4. **Use strong, unique passwords.** Avoid using your birthdate, pet's name, or "password123". Use a password manager if necessary.
5. **Update software regularly.** Keep your phone OS, computer OS, and web browsers updated to patch security vulnerabilities.
6. **Be skeptical of urgent requests.** If an email or message demands immediate action (e.g., "Your account will be suspended!"), it is likely a phishing scam. Pause and verify independently.
7. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.** Do not check your bank account or pay fees while connected to an unsecured café or airport Wi-Fi network.
8. **Know how to report incidents.** If you are a victim of cybercrime, report it immediately to the **National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)** or call the **Helpline at 1930**.
