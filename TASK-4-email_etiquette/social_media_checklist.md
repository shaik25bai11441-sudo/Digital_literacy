# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

### ✅ The Do's
1. **Do audit your digital footprint:** Regularly search your name to see what potential employers or professors might find.
2. **Do curate a professional network:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, professors, and industry professionals in the tech field.
3. **Do pause before posting:** Assume everything you post is permanent, even if it is on a private story or account.
4. **Do engage constructively:** Participate in academic or hobby-based communities (like GitHub or Stack Overflow) by asking good questions and encouraging peers.
5. **Do review privacy settings:** Frequently update your privacy controls to ensure personal life updates are only visible to close friends and family.

### ❌ The Don'ts
1. **Don't overshare personal details:** Avoid posting your exact location, phone number, or photos of sensitive documents (like your student ID or tickets).
2. **Don't vent about professors or peers:** Never use public platforms to complain about your university, coursework, or specific individuals.
3. **Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling:** Avoid leaving aggressive, hateful, or mocking comments on others' posts.
4. **Don't plagiarize content:** Never claim someone else's digital work, art, or code as your own without proper attribution.
5. **Don't click suspicious links:** Be wary of viral trends or DMs that require you to click unknown links, as they are often phishing attempts.
